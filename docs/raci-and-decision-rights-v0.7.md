Project: First Nations Governance Commons  
Initial developer/custodian: Barayamal / Dean Foley  
Purpose: Clarify who is responsible, accountable, consulted, and informed.

## Role Definitions

| Role | Description |
|---|---|
| Barayamal / Dean Foley | Initial developer, custodian, coordinator, and open commons maintainer. |
| First Nations Advisors | Review legitimacy, cultural safety, data sovereignty, offline participation, and accountability. |

| Local Admins | Manage local Decidim spaces, content, moderation, and updates. |

| Technical Maintainer | Installs, configures, secures, and maintains Decidim. |
| Partner/Funder | Provides support, responds to commitments, but does not control process rules. |

## Decision Rights

### Barayamal Can Decide

- Public fake-data demo structure.
- Open commons documentation.
- Initial product roadmap.
- Technical approach for demo/sandbox.
- Which advisors to invite.

### Barayamal Should Not Decide Alone

- Real community process authority.
- Real eligibility or voting rules.
- Real cultural safety boundaries.
- Real community data ownership.
- Real partner access to community records.

### Community Authority Must Decide

- Whether a real process is authorised.
- Who can participate and vote.
- What can be public.
- What must remain restricted or offline.
- What outcomes are official.
- Who can access/export/delete community data.

## Anti-Capture Rule

Partners can fund, respond, and support. They must not control eligibility, moderation, cultural boundaries, data access, or outcomes.
