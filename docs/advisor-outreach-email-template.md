## Longer Email

Subject: Request for advisor feedback: Barayamal First Nations Governance Commons demo

Hi [Name],

I am preparing a public fake-data demo for a project called **First Nations Governance Commons**.

The idea is to create First Nations-controlled civic infrastructure using Decidim, an open-source participatory democracy framework. The goal is to support community mandate, participatory budgeting, consent-style processes, agreement tracking, service accountability, and evidence-based advocacy.

The public demo is deliberately fake-data only. It is designed to show the model safely before any real community pilot.

The key concept is the **Mandate Record**. Instead of just collecting feedback, each process should show:

- Who authorised the process.
- Who could participate.
- How online and offline input was gathered.
- What the community said.
- What decision was made.
- What conditions apply.
- Who must act.
- What changed.
- What remains unresolved.

I am looking for advisor feedback before moving from static public demo to a Decidim sandbox.

Could you review the demo and comment on:

1. Whether the process model feels legitimate.
2. What cultural safety risks need stronger controls.
3. What data sovereignty/privacy rules are needed.
4. Whether offline participation is treated properly.
5. Whether the accountability/evidence model would be useful.
6. What should change before a real pilot.
