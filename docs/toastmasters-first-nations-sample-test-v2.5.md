# Toastmasters First Nations Sample Test v2.5

Mode: controlled sample initiative only.

## Purpose

Use Toastmasters First Nations to test whether the Governance Commons model can support a First Nations initiative or organisation joining without confusing public coordination with real membership, official paperwork, payment, or community authority.

## Source Facts Used

The Barayamal page describes Toastmasters First Nations as a proposed online club in formation for First Nations leadership, storytelling, confidence, and public speaking. It says the online demo is Monday 15 June 2026, 6:30-7:30 PM AEST, that the club is not chartered yet, that the website form is intake only, and that official membership starts only after official Toastmasters paperwork, sponsorship/payment reconciliation, and charter acceptance.

The page also states a 20 founding member charter target, a 17 non-dual member gate, and an officer slate needing a President, one Vice President, and Secretary or Secretary/Treasurer held by three different people.

Toastmasters official sources also describe 20 prospective members over 18 for starting a club, charter paperwork requirements, and minimum officer requirements.

## What To Test In Governance Commons

1. Public initiative profile renders clearly.
2. Tracks do not prematurely count people as members.
3. Organisation supporter/referrer path exists.
4. Charter gates can be shown with fake/sample counts.
5. Private fields are excluded from the public sample.
6. Official paperwork remains separate.
7. Real pilot and real member data require separate approvals.

## Decidim Sample Shape

- Assembly: Toastmasters First Nations sample initiative.
- Page: public overview, purpose, values, FAQ, data boundary.
- Meetings: online demo agenda and meeting rhythm.
- Survey: fake track selection and access-to-information questions only.
- Proposals: club culture, meeting themes, and support ideas.
- Accountability: charter target, 17 non-dual gate, officer slate, paperwork, sponsorship, and official submission gates.

## Not Allowed In The Public Sample

- Real addresses.
- Real phone numbers.
- Real email addresses.
- Real access needs.
- Real age confirmations.
- Real Toastmasters member numbers.
- Real payment/card details.
- Official applications or signatures.
- Private club or organisation records.
- Claims that the sample creates official Toastmasters membership.

## Recommended Decision

Proceed with Toastmasters First Nations as a controlled public coordination and readiness sample. Do not use the public demo as the real intake form or official Toastmasters record system.
