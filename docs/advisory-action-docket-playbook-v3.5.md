# Advisory Action Docket Playbook v3.5

Initial developer/custodian: Barayamal / Dean Foley

## Purpose

Use this public-safe docket after Evidence Vault & Advisory Review. It turns Dean Foley / Barayamal advisory findings into action items that a private authority meeting can act on.

## Boundary

Dean Foley / Barayamal may act as advisory and trusted reviewer by raising findings, questions, recommendations, and escalations, but cannot approve the pilot, override First Nations authority, replace privacy or Indigenous Data Sovereignty review, approve official Toastmasters records, or receive unrestricted private evidence.

Every advisory finding must become a public-safe action item with an owner type, evidence needed, acceptance proof, escalation route, and authority decision motion before any real private pilot action proceeds.

Do not open real intake, activate a private workspace, share private evidence, hand data to a provider, or scale the pilot until the advisory action docket is closed or deliberately carried as private meeting conditions by the approved First Nations authority group.

## How to use it

1. Keep this public package fake/sample only.
2. Table Dean's advisory note as input, not approval.
3. Convert every unresolved advisory finding into an action item.
4. Assign owner type, evidence needed, acceptance proof, and escalation route.
5. Prepare authority motions for blocked, hold, and required items.
6. Move private evidence requests into the approved private vault.
7. Record whether each motion is ready, held, blocked, or carried as a condition.
8. Publish only aggregate public status.

## Required private meeting inputs

- Authority quorum and delegation record.
- IDS/data governance decision.
- Privacy decision.
- Private environment proof.
- Role access and revoke test.
- Official Toastmasters boundary.
- Support and incident route.
- Monitoring and closeout conditions.

## Public status rule

Publish owner type, status, blocker class, and next route only. Never publish real people, official records, access logs, member numbers, incidents, payments, signatures, access needs, or private decisions.
