# Private Pilot Implementation Playbook v2.8

Mode: public fake/sample template only  
Sample initiative: Toastmasters First Nations  
Initial developer/custodian: Barayamal / Dean Foley  

## Purpose

This playbook turns the v2.6 readiness gates and v2.7 operations controls into a practical private pilot preparation path.

It does not authorise real data in the public demo. Real member, contact, access-need, payment, official Toastmasters, meeting-link, incident, authority, consent, and deletion records must stay in a separate approved private environment.

## Implementation Rule

Use the public package for review, training, partner briefings, and fake-data rehearsal only.

Use the private environment for any real intake, contact, consent, access support, official handoff, meeting coordination, incident response, and closeout evidence.

## Prerequisites

- A named pilot authority is approved.
- Barayamal / Dean Foley is confirmed as initial developer-custodian or the role is reassigned in writing.
- The v2.6 Private Pilot Readiness gates are reviewed.
- The v2.7 Pilot Operations Control flow is rehearsed with fake data.
- The private environment decision record is approved.
- The minimum data schema is locked.
- The access register is approved.
- The public evidence rule is understood by every operator.

## 30-Day Preparation Sequence

### Days 1-3: Authority And Scope

- Confirm the pilot authority.
- Confirm who can pause or stop the pilot.
- Confirm what First Nations Toastmasters is testing.
- Confirm what must stay outside Governance Commons.
- Confirm the public demo is not a system of record.

### Days 4-7: Private Environment

- Select the private workspace or hosted Decidim pathway.
- Confirm admin owners and MFA.
- Confirm backup, export, and deletion path.
- Confirm no shared admin accounts.
- Confirm access review cadence.

### Days 8-12: Data And Consent

- Approve the minimum data schema.
- Remove any unnecessary free-text fields.
- Approve privacy and consent wording.
- Confirm withdrawal and correction route.
- Confirm access support handling.

### Days 13-16: Official Handoff

- Confirm official Toastmasters form owner.
- Confirm member numbers, signatures, official applications, charter roster, and payments stay outside the public demo.
- Confirm only approved status summaries can return to Governance Commons.
- Confirm the charter member count is not mixed with demo-first or supporter-only contacts.

### Days 17-21: Dry Run

- Run an end-to-end fake-data dry run.
- Test founder intake, demo-first intake, supporter routing, access support, official handoff, incident pause, evidence redaction, and closeout.
- Log defects privately.
- Do not start real intake until critical defects are closed.

### Days 22-26: Go/No-Go Review

- Review required implementation actions.
- Review required environment controls.
- Review never-public fields.
- Review access register.
- Review incident and deletion paths.
- Decide: proceed, hold, or stop.

### Days 27-30: Controlled Start

- Open only the approved intake path.
- Use only the locked fields.
- Keep public updates aggregate and redacted.
- Review access weekly.
- Close out immediately if a no-go trigger occurs.

## No-Go Triggers

- Real private data appears in the public demo, public repo, public screenshots, public CSVs, or fake-data Decidim sandbox.
- Official Toastmasters applications, signatures, member numbers, payment/card details, or meeting links are stored in the public package.
- The private environment has no named owner, MFA, backup/export, incident route, or deletion rule.
- A participant cannot withdraw, ask what is stored, or request correction.
- Access needs or incident details are copied into public evidence.
- Supporters, mentors, demo-first attendees, or unsure contacts are counted as charter-ready founders without confirmation.

## Evidence Required

- Private authority note.
- Private environment decision record.
- Minimum data schema.
- Access register.
- Privacy and consent notice.
- Official Toastmasters handoff rule.
- Fake-data dry-run log.
- Go/no-go decision record.
- Closeout and deletion evidence template.

## Public Evidence Allowed

- Fake IDs.
- Aggregate counts.
- Redacted status summaries.
- Public templates.
- Screenshots checked against the redaction rule.
- Links to official public source pages.

## Public Evidence Excluded

- Real names.
- Contact details.
- Meeting links.
- Access needs.
- Incident details.
- Official Toastmasters applications.
- Signatures.
- Member numbers.
- Payment, card, receipt, bank, or sponsorship reconciliation details.
- Real authority records.

## Recommended Next Step

Run the v2.8 implementation pack as a private readiness sprint. Do not open real intake until the required implementation actions, required environment controls, never-public field rules, and access register are approved.
