# Pilot Process Rules: 2026 Community Priorities And Budget

Date: 2026-06-04  
Status: v0.1 working draft  
Platform: First Nations Governance Commons  
Initial developer/custodian: Barayamal / Dean Foley

## 1. Process Purpose

The 2026 Community Priorities and Budget pilot tests whether a First Nations community can use Decidim to:

- Share information.
- Gather proposals.
- Include offline and online participation.
- Prioritise community ideas.
- Publish results.
- Track accepted priorities.
- Produce a Mandate Record and evidence pack.

## 2. Core Question

What should the community prioritise for 2026 funding, advocacy, service improvement, or collective action?

## 3. Authorising Body

To be confirmed before launch.

The authorising body must approve:

- Process purpose.
- Participation rules.
- Eligibility rules.
- Decision method.
- Publication rules.
- Data rules.
- Cultural safety rules.

## 4. Decidim Components

Use native Decidim features first:

- Public information page.
- Participatory process.
- Proposals.
- Meetings.
- Survey.
- Voting/support.
- Accountability.
- Newsletters/notifications.
- Pages or blog updates.

## 5. Participation Methods

Participants may contribute through:

- Online proposals.
- Online survey.
- Online meeting.
- Offline yarning circles.
- Paper forms.
- Phone or assisted participation where available.
- Facilitator-uploaded summaries.

Offline input must be visible as official participation.

## 6. Eligibility

Eligibility must be confirmed before launch.

Possible models:

- Open public input, but community-only voting/support.
- Member-only participation.
- Residents plus Traditional Owners.
- Youth-only stream for youth priorities.
- Elders/cultural authority advice stream.
- Hybrid model with different rights for different roles.

The chosen model must be written in plain language before participation begins.

## 7. Process Timeline

Suggested pilot timeline:

| Phase | Duration | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Setup | 2 weeks | Rules, pages, survey, proposal categories, launch checklist. |
| Information | 1 week | Public explainer and meeting schedule. |
| Input | 2-3 weeks | Proposals, surveys, yarning circles, phone/paper input. |
| Review | 1 week | Merge duplicates, check eligibility, categorise proposals. |
| Prioritisation | 1-2 weeks | Voting/support or agreed prioritisation method. |
| Decision | 1 week | Final priorities approved by authority. |
| Publication | 1 week | Results, Mandate Record, accountability tracker. |
| Follow-up | Monthly or quarterly | Progress updates and evidence pack. |

## 8. Proposal Rules

Each proposal should include:

- Title.
- Summary.
- Problem or opportunity.
- Who benefits.
- Suggested action.
- Location or community area if relevant.
- Estimated cost if known.
- Whether it needs partner/government/funder action.
- Any cultural or privacy concerns.

Admins may merge duplicates, request clarification, or mark proposals out of scope according to published rules.

## 9. Survey Rules

The survey should be short and plain-language.

Suggested topics:

- Top community priorities.
- Service gaps.
- Budget/funding preferences.
- Youth priorities.
- Elders/community wellbeing priorities.
- Barriers to participation.
- Preferred follow-up method.

Avoid collecting unnecessary personal or sensitive data.

## 10. Voting Or Support Rules

The final model must be chosen before launch.

Options:

- Support proposals, no formal vote.
- Ranked priorities.
- Participatory budget allocation.
- Member-only vote.
- Youth stream and general stream.
- Advisory vote followed by community authority decision.

The process must explain:

- Who can vote/support.
- Whether results are binding or advisory.
- How ties are handled.
- How conflicts of interest are handled.
- Whether late or offline votes/support are accepted.

## 11. Offline Yarning Circle Import

Each offline session should record:

- Date.
- Location or format.
- Facilitator.
- Number of participants, if appropriate.
- Main themes.
- Proposals raised.
- Concerns or objections.
- Publication limits.
- Follow-up actions.

The upload should not include names or sensitive details unless consent and process rules allow it.

## 12. Cultural Safety

The process must define:

- What should stay offline.
- What should be member-only.
- What should be restricted.
- What can be public.
- Who reviews cultural concerns.
- How content is removed or redacted if needed.

Default rule:

Do not upload restricted cultural material into Decidim.

## 13. Moderation And Disputes

Moderation should cover:

- Abuse.
- Racism.
- Harassment.
- Privacy breaches.
- Misinformation.
- Duplicate proposals.
- Defamation risk.
- Cultural safety.
- Conflicts of interest.

Participants need a clear pathway to request correction, appeal moderation, or raise a process concern.

## 14. Accountability Rules

Accepted priorities must become accountability items.

Each item should include:

- Priority name.
- Responsible party.
- Status.
- Next step.
- Due date or review date.
- Evidence/update notes.
- Partner response if relevant.

Suggested statuses:

- Accepted.
- In progress.
- Funded.
- Awaiting partner response.
- Blocked.
- Completed.
- Escalated.

## 15. Mandate Record

At the end of the process, publish or store a Mandate Record showing:

- What was asked.
- Who authorised the process.
- Who participated.
- How offline input was included.
- What information was provided.
- What the community said.
- What decision was made.
- What conditions apply.
- Who must act.
- What remains unresolved.

Publication level:

- Public.
- Member-only.
- Restricted.
- Redacted.
- Confidential.

## 16. Launch Gates

The pilot should not launch until:

- Authorising body is confirmed.
- Data rules are approved.
- Cultural safety boundaries are approved.
- Eligibility is clear.
- Moderators/admins are ready.
- Offline participation process is tested.
- Decidim process is tested on mobile.
- Backup/restore is tested.
- Privacy notice is drafted.
- Evidence pack template is ready.

## 17. Pilot Success Criteria

The pilot succeeds if:

- People understand the process.
- Online and offline input both count.
- Local admins can manage the process.
- Results are published clearly.
- Accepted priorities become tracked actions.
- A Mandate Record is produced.
- Lessons become reusable templates for the commons.
