# Funder And Partner Brief

Status: v0.1 briefing note  
Project: First Nations Governance Commons  
Initial developer/custodian: Barayamal / Dean Foley  
Demo mode: public demo with fake/sample data only

## Summary

First Nations Governance Commons is a Barayamal-initiated, First Nations-controlled civic infrastructure project using Decidim to support community mandate, participatory budgeting, consent-style processes, agreement tracking, service accountability, and evidence-based advocacy.

The public demo shows the model safely using fake/sample data.

## What Makes It Different

This is not just an engagement website.

The platform is designed to produce a **Mandate Record**:

- Who authorised the process.
- Who could participate.
- How online and offline input was gathered.
- What the community said.
- What decision was made.
- What conditions apply.
- Who must act.
- What changed.
- What remains unresolved.

## Why Fund It

The project supports:

- First Nations governance capacity.
- Digital inclusion.
- Community-controlled data infrastructure.
- Transparent funding priorities.
- Better partner accountability.
- Youth digital stewardship.
- Evidence for advocacy, funding, and policy reform.
- Reusable open-source civic technology.

## What Funding Should Cover

| Work Package | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Governance design | Charters, process rules, partner boundaries, launch gates. |
| Decidim sandbox | Working fake-data sandbox and process templates. |
| Offline participation | Yarning circle templates, facilitator workflows, import process. |
| Evidence system | Mandate Record and evidence pack exports. |
| Privacy and security | Privacy review, hosting, backups, access controls. |
| Training | Local admins, youth stewards, facilitators, moderators. |
| Evaluation | Pilot review, legitimacy review, repeatability assessment. |
| Open commons | Public templates, documentation, and reusable modules. |

## Partner Role

Partners can:

- Provide information.
- Respond to community priorities.
- Update commitment status.
- Fund infrastructure and training.
- Support implementation.

Partners must not:

- Control community eligibility.
- Control moderation.
- Override outcomes.
- Access restricted cultural or member data.
- Turn the platform into tokenistic consultation.

## Recommended Funding Path

1. Fund public demo review and Decidim sandbox.
2. Fund one controlled fake-data sandbox with advisor review.
3. Fund one real pilot only after governance, data, privacy, cultural safety, and local admin readiness gates pass.
4. Fund reusable open commons materials so other communities can adapt the model.

## Success Measures

- Decidim sandbox configured with fake/sample data.
- Governance and data charters reviewed.
- Offline participation workflow tested.
- Mandate Record template validated.
- Evidence pack useful to advisors/funders.
- Real pilot go/no-go decision made transparently.

## Core Guardrail

Funding can support the infrastructure, but it must not buy control over community authority, process rules, participant data, or outcomes.
