# First Nations Governance Commons: Governance Charter Draft

Date: 2026-06-04  
Status: v0.1 working draft  
Initial developer/custodian: Barayamal / Dean Foley  
Purpose: Define who can authorise, participate, decide, moderate, publish, review, and hold partners accountable.

## 1. Charter Purpose

This charter sets the minimum governance rules for using the First Nations Governance Commons.

It exists to protect:

- First Nations authority.
- Community decision legitimacy.
- Cultural safety.
- Offline participation.
- Data sovereignty.
- Public accountability.
- Local control over local processes.

## 2. Core Principle

Barayamal / Dean Foley may build and maintain the platform, but local communities control their own decision-making authority, data, participation rules, and cultural boundaries.

## 3. Governance Bodies

| Body | Role |
|---|---|
| Platform Custodian | Barayamal / Dean Foley initially builds, maintains, documents, and coordinates the platform. |
| First Nations Governance Council | Advisory/review body for platform principles, safety rules, data sovereignty, and anti-capture controls. |
| Community Authority | Local body or group that authorises a specific community process. |
| Local Admins | People approved to manage the local Decidim space, content, moderation, records, and updates. |
| Facilitators | People who support yarning circles, offline input, translation, accessibility, and meeting records. |
| Technical Maintainers | Developers and operators responsible for code, hosting, security, backups, and releases. |
| Partners | Governments, funders, companies, researchers, universities, or service providers with response-only roles. |

## 4. Decision Rights

| Decision Area | Who Decides |
|---|---|
| Core technical build | Platform Custodian with technical maintainers. |
| Local process purpose | Community Authority. |
| Eligibility and voting rights | Community Authority. |
| Cultural boundaries | Relevant cultural authority. |
| Public publication of local outcomes | Community Authority or delegated local admins. |
| Shared commons templates | Platform Custodian, Governance Council, and maintainer review. |
| Partner access | Community Authority. |
| Data export/deletion | Community Authority under the Data Sovereignty Charter. |
| Moderation appeals | Local process rules, with escalation pathway if needed. |

## 5. Participation Spaces

The platform may include:

- Public spaces.
- Member-only spaces.
- Elders or cultural authority spaces.
- Youth spaces.
- Board or committee spaces.
- Partner response spaces.
- Admin-only spaces.
- Restricted cultural spaces where explicitly approved.

Default rule:

Public transparency is valuable, but not everything belongs in public.

## 6. Process Approval Requirements

Before any real process launches, the following must be documented:

- Process name and purpose.
- Authorising body.
- Decision authority.
- Eligibility rules.
- Participation methods.
- Offline participation method.
- Timeline.
- Moderation rules.
- Data collection rules.
- Cultural safety boundaries.
- Decision method.
- Accountability method.
- Publication rules.
- Appeal/correction pathway.

## 7. Partner Boundaries

Partners may:

- Provide information.
- Respond to questions.
- Upload public progress updates if approved.
- Receive evidence packs where permitted.
- Be assigned accountability actions.

Partners must not:

- Control community eligibility.
- Control moderation.
- Override outcomes.
- Access restricted records without approval.
- Edit community decisions.
- Use the platform to create tokenistic consultation.

## 8. Moderation Rules

Each process must have moderation rules covering:

- Abuse and harassment.
- Racism.
- Privacy breaches.
- Misinformation.
- Lateral violence.
- Conflicts of interest.
- Duplicate or spam proposals.
- Defamation risk.
- Cultural safety concerns.
- Appeals and correction requests.

Moderation must be documented and reviewable.

## 9. Offline Participation

Offline participation must be treated as official participation.

Accepted methods may include:

- Yarning circles.
- Paper forms.
- Phone calls.
- Facilitator interviews.
- Outreach sessions.
- Community meetings.
- Assisted digital participation.

Offline records must show:

- Date.
- Location or format.
- Facilitator.
- Participation method.
- Summary of input.
- Any consent or publication limits.
- Whether input is public, member-only, restricted, or confidential.

## 10. Mandate Record Requirement

Each major process should produce a Mandate Record recording:

- Authority.
- Eligibility.
- Participation methods.
- Information provided.
- What was heard.
- Decision method.
- Outcome.
- Conditions.
- Responsible parties.
- Publication status.
- Review pathway.

## 11. Review Cycle

This charter should be reviewed:

- Before the first pilot.
- After the first pilot.
- Every 6 months during active build.
- Whenever a major governance, privacy, cultural safety, or technical incident occurs.
