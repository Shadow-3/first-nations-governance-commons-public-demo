# Toastmasters First Nations Private Pilot Closeout Plan v3.0

Release: v3.0 Private Pilot Closeout Decision  
Initial developer/custodian: Barayamal / Dean Foley  
Sample initiative: Toastmasters First Nations  
Mode: controlled public sample and private-pilot closeout template only

## Positioning

Toastmasters First Nations remains a controlled sample for testing whether First Nations organisations and initiatives can join the Governance Commons model. The public demo is not official Toastmasters membership, payment, meeting, application, charter, or member-record infrastructure.

## Closeout Objective

The closeout decides whether the controlled sample should proceed, hold, no-go, remain public-demo-only, move to official Toastmasters processes, build a private Decidim pilot workspace, seek provider support, or prepare a second controlled pilot.

## Required Proof Before Proceeding

- Closeout authority is named.
- Intake is closed or formally extended.
- Private records inventory is complete.
- Withdrawal, correction, access support, and incident records are resolved.
- Official Toastmasters handoff remains outside the public package.
- Access register is revoked or re-approved.
- Public summary has redaction QA.
- Community authority pathway is clear before wider First Nations use.

## Decision Options

Use the scale decision options CSV to compare proceed, hold, no-go, public-demo-only, official handoff, private Decidim workspace, provider quote, community governance approval, public learning note, ownership transfer, archive, and future EOI options.

## Public Wording Rule

Do not imply official club status, official membership, charter acceptance, public intake, or wider First Nations authority unless the correct official or community authority pathway has approved it.

## Recommended Next Step

Use this closeout plan to turn the first controlled sample into an evidence-backed decision. Keep all real closeout evidence in the approved private environment.
