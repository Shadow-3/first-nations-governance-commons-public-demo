# Organisation And Initiative Onboarding Playbook v2.5

Mode: public demo with fake/sample organisation and initiative data only.

Initial developer/custodian: Barayamal / Dean Foley.

## Review Finding

The v2.4 package controls provider delivery, but it does not yet provide a complete path for a First Nations organisation or initiative to join the Governance Commons model. Organisations can appear as partners, reviewers, funders, supporters, or community authorities, but there is no dedicated onboarding layer that separates public initiative coordination from real membership, private contact data, official applications, payment, or community authority.

## What v2.5 Adds

v2.5 adds Organisation Onboarding, using Toastmasters First Nations as a controlled sample initiative.

It adds:

- A sample First Nations initiative profile.
- Five join tracks.
- Twelve onboarding and readiness gates.
- Ten data-boundary rules.
- Seven Decidim space/component mappings.
- A generated onboarding brief.
- A public test pack for whether First Nations organisations can join as hosts, supporters, referrers, mentors, reviewers, or future community authorities.

## Controlled Sample Rule

The Toastmasters First Nations sample must not be used as the real intake system. Real intake stays on the Barayamal page and official Toastmasters paperwork. This sample can model public coordination, readiness gates, sample Decidim components, and governance boundaries only.

## Recommended Test Sequence

1. Confirm the initiative can be shown publicly without implying official membership or charter acceptance.
2. Confirm track choices are clear: founding member now, demo first, supporter/mentor, organisation supporter, or not sure.
3. Confirm supporter-only and demo-first people are not counted as charter-ready members.
4. Confirm private fields are excluded from public demo data.
5. Confirm charter-readiness gates can be displayed using fake/sample counts.
6. Confirm organisation supporters can help without controlling member data or official acceptance.
7. Confirm any real pilot requires separate authority, privacy, data, cultural safety, moderation, hosting, and support approvals.
